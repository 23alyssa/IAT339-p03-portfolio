I’M ALYSSA!

I am an enthusiastic design student looking to make the world a little better using the power of visuals. I have a skills in graphic and web design as well as UI/UX.

A Little About my Journey

In my life I have had two big passions; art and technology. As a little girl I loved art and would always be doodling on paper or drawing my favourite Disney characters in my sketchbook. While growing up in the age of technology, I was fascinated by computers and the limitless possibilities that they can provide in society. Through the Interactive Arts & Technology program at Simon Fraser University, I have found a way to connect both these passions, and continue to grow as a designer.

At the university level, I have gained technical and theoretical experience in the area of graphic design and visual communication. In several projects I have been tasked with creating design ethos relying on principles such as hierarchy, balance and contrast to convey a particular experience. Using Adobe programs such as InDesign and Illustrator I have designed two print magazine layouts, a logo/branding concept, and 2 web design layouts.

Outside of my education, I am working as a volunteer web designer for Emerge GX, an education start-up, that provides live and recorded workshops with a focus on fostering peer connections. As the only web designer on the team, I am responsible for creating the flow of the user, initial sketches and wireframes, branded assets, and webpage templates. I have created multiple commerce web pages, as well as a community forum and user access to courses. At the moment my designs are being handed off to developers for implementation on the website.

My Design Process

01

Ideation

Formalizing a design concept and project scope is one of the most important first steps. This includes building a sitemap, creating user flows, and wireframes.

02

Iteration

Finding the best design solution rarely happens on the first try. Its takes a lot of experimentation and iteration to find the best solution for the project,

02

Implementation

Going beyond the means of the project to prototyping, developing and testing the designs with users ensures it can be applied to real world scenarios.
